Cindr App — Complete Product Description

Cindr
Find your perfect match. Movie edition.
PRODUCT BRIEF — v1.0
What it is
Cindr is a swipe-based movie and series discovery web app. Instead of scrolling through endless Netflix grids, users swipe through personalised movie cards one at a time — like Tinder, but for films. Swipe right to watch the trailer, swipe left to skip. No more decision paralysis.
Core user flow
1
Land & choose
User visits the site. Can start immediately without an account, or sign up to unlock saving features.
2
Language selection
Pick up to 3 preferred languages from a curated list (Hindi, Korean, English, Tamil, Japanese, Spanish, Turkish etc.) shown with flags.
3
Taste questions
A short onboarding quiz infers preferred genres and moods — not overwhelming, just enough to seed the recommendation engine.
4
Swipe
Movie cards appear one by one — poster, title, and a short summary. Swipe right = interested. Swipe left = skip. That's it.
5
Trailer + save
Swipe right triggers a trailer popup (YouTube embed) plus "Where to Watch" info, a Save for Later button, and a Favourite button.
Data source
TMDB API (free)
Movie/series data, posters, trailers, cast, ratings, genres, 292 language filters, and a powerful Discover endpoint.
Watchmode API
"Where to Watch" — which streaming platform has it. Paid at scale but free tier works for MVP.
Genres supported
Action Thriller Drama Comedy Romance Horror Sci-Fi Mystery Fantasy Animation Documentary Crime Adventure History War Family Music Western + TV genres
Retention & social features
Streaks
Weekly swipe streaks. Not punishing — missing one day doesn't reset everything.
Badges
Earn badges like "Genre Hopper", "World Cinema", "Night Owl". Fun, shareable, costs nothing to build.
Friends
Add or invite friends via link. See what they saved. "Compare taste" feature shows overlap.
Reactions
After watching: 🔥 Loved / 👍 Good / 😐 Meh / ❌ Not for me. Feeds back into recommendations.
Watchlist
Saved movies feel like an inbox. Mark as watched to close the loop and re-engage users.
New drops
"3 new Korean thrillers added this week" — personalised nudges that feel worth opening.
